Color

A color (hue) is the visual perception that enables one to differentiate otherwise identical objects.

The element of color is such a huge topic that it really can't be discussed and understood all at one time. There is just something in the human soul that can distinctly tell when something is visually pleasing...and when it is not. Your art will look and feel so much better when effectively using colors together. Here is a list of color schemes to choose from:

  • Analogous - Colors found adjacent to each other on the color wheel (blue and green)
  • Complementary - Colors found opposite of each other on the color wheel (blue and orange)
  • Split-Complementary - A color and the two colors next to its complement (yellow, blue-violet, red-violet)
  • Triadic - Three colors spaced evenly apart on the color wheel
  • Monochromatic - A single color with its tints, tones, and shades

"Color is a matter of taste and of sensitivity." - Edouard Manet
